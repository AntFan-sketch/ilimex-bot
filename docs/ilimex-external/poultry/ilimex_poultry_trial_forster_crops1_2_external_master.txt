Title: Ilimex Poultry Trials – A.J. Forster Farm (Crops 1 & 2)
Document Type: Trial Summary (External – Core)
Sector: Poultry
Use: External Bot
Last Updated: 2026

Overview:
Ilimex conducted controlled poultry trials at A.J. Forster’s commercial farm, comparing a house fitted with the Ilimex Flufence system against a control house operating under standard conditions.

Across two consecutive crops, the trials demonstrate consistent improvements in flock performance, particularly in mortality reduction, bird liveability, and overall economic return.

Trial Design:
- House 2: Ilimex Flufence installed
- House 5: Control (no Ilimex system)
- Flock size: ~21,560 birds per house
- Same breed, stocking density, and management practices
- Environmental monitoring via Hotraco Fortica systems
- Parameters tracked: CO₂, water intake, ventilation, pressure, temperature

Environmental Validation:
Environmental data shows both houses operated under closely matched conditions across all key parameters.

This confirms that observed performance differences are not due to environmental variation, but are associated with the Ilimex system.

Key Results (Consistent Across Crops):
- ~0.5% reduction in mortality
- Equivalent to approximately 110 additional birds surviving per flock
- Repeatable across both Crop 1 and Crop 2

Economic Impact (Example Scenario):
- Approx. £330 additional revenue per flock (based on ~£3 per bird)
- Historical performance improvement:
  - Crop 1: +10p per bird
  - Crop 2: +5p per bird

- Typical annual impact:
  - ~£11,000 per poultry house per year (based on 7 crops and average performance uplift)

Performance vs Historical Baseline:
The Ilimex-treated house delivered:
- The best and second-best performing crops ever recorded for that house
- Significant improvement relative to its own historical averages

The control house was already one of the strongest-performing houses on the farm, making this comparison particularly robust.

Weaker Flock Recovery Signal:
In Crop 2, the Ilimex house was intentionally stocked with younger and lower-quality chicks.

Despite this disadvantage:
- Performance improved over the crop
- The flock recovered strongly relative to expectations

This indicates that improved air quality may help stabilise weaker or more vulnerable flocks.

What This Means for Farmers:
- Improved flock survivability
- More consistent performance across crops
- Potential uplift in bird weight and liveability
- Increased margin per bird and per house
- Greater resilience in challenging conditions

Technical Insight:
The Ilimex Flufence system is designed to improve air quality within poultry houses by reducing airborne pathogen load and stabilising environmental conditions.

The observed improvements in mortality and performance are consistent with this mechanism.

Validation Status:
- Results are based on controlled, side-by-side commercial trials
- Consistent performance observed across two crops
- A third crop is planned using identical flocks to further validate results
